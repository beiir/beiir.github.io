Tutorial Schedule

15 minutes Part One: Introduction
1) Welcome and introductions;
2) Exercise: How biased are you?
45 minutes Part Two: Theory and Background
1) An overview of the space and spectrum of different cognitive bias ((Azzopardi, 2021, Liu, 2023)
2) Rational Choice and Expected Utility Theory (von Neuman and Morgenstern, 1947)
3) Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases (Tversky and Kahneman, 1974)
  • a) Availability, Anchoring and Adjustment;
  • b) Thinking Fast and Thinking Slow;
  • c) Fast and Frugal (Gigerenzer and Todd, 1999);
  • d) Bounded Rationality and Satisficing (Simon, 1972);
  • e) Prospect Theory (Kahneman and Tversky, 1979): Loss aversion, reference dependence, diminishing sensitivity
    		•
    90 minutes Part Three - Biases in Search
    1) Deathly decisions when searching for Health and Medical information online
  • a) Do people seek information to confirm their hypothesis? (White 2013 & 2014, Kesselman et al 2008)
  • b) How does anchoring and availability influence people’s health decisions? Pogacar et al 2017, Ghenai et al 2020)
  • c) Can Exposure and order effects lead to people to accept misinformation? (Abualsand & Smucker, 2019)
    • 2) Mitigating Cognitive Biases during Query Formulation (Pothirattanachaikul, 2019 & 2020, Yamamoto, 2018)
  • a) Can Priming and Anchoring Biases be used to enhance critical thinking?
  • b) How should we mitigate priming and anchoring biases when they are negatively affecting users' judgments?
    • 3) Trust and Position Bias in Web Search (Keane et al, 2008, Murphy, 2006, Wu et al , 2012; Craswel et al 2008; Zipf 1949)
  • a) What is the impact of Primacy/Recency Effects and Order Effects on web search behavior?
  • b) Could people be subscribing to thePrinciple of Least Effort? Is this rational, or irrational?
    • 4) Cognitive Biases in Evaluation and Assessment
  • a) What is the impact of ordering and threshold priming effects when collecting judgements? (Scholar et al, 2013, Kazai et al 2012)
  • b) How does anchoring, bandwagon effects, decoy effects, etc. skew and distort crowd judgements? (Eickohff, 2018, Draws et al, 2021)
  • c) Are people’s satisfaction judgements in whole session evaluation dependent on some reference points? (Liu & Han, 2020, Chenet al 2023)
    • 90 minutes Part Four - Tips, Challenges and Future Directions
    1) Study Design and Methodological Challenges
    2) Practical Implications for IR System Design and Evaluation
    3) Future directions and challenges in bias prediction, bias mitigation, and user modeling in new modalities of interactions (e.g. LLMs, GenAI, etc.)
